ACCOUNTS PAYABLE POLICY — DUPLICATE INVOICE DETECTION AND CONTROLS
Document Reference: AP-POL-002
Issued by: Finance Compliance, Internal Controls Division
Effective Date: January 2024 | Review Date: January 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the controls for detecting, holding, and resolving
duplicate invoice submissions from vendors. Duplicate payments represent
a significant source of financial loss and vendor relationship risk.

2. DEFINITION OF A DUPLICATE INVOICE
An invoice is classified as a potential duplicate if any of the following
conditions are met:

  a) The same vendor submits an invoice for the same PO reference within
     7 calendar days of a previous invoice from that vendor on the same PO.

  b) The invoice number is identical to a previously submitted invoice from
     the same vendor, regardless of date.

  c) The invoice amount matches a previously paid invoice within a 5%
     tolerance, same vendor, same PO reference, within a 30-day window.

3. AUTOMATIC PAYMENT HOLD
Any invoice flagged as a potential duplicate must be placed on automatic
payment hold. The hold may not be released without written confirmation
from the AP Manager.

4. VENDOR NOTIFICATION
The vendor must be notified within 2 business days of the flag being raised.
The notification must request:
  — Confirmation of whether the second invoice is intentional
  — Evidence if the second invoice covers a separate delivery or service
  — A written withdrawal of the duplicate if confirmed as an error

5. RESOLUTION TIMELINE
Duplicate flags must be resolved within 10 business days. If unresolved,
the invoice is rejected and returned to the vendor.

6. FINANCIAL IMPACT TRACKING
All prevented duplicate payments must be logged as AP savings in the
monthly controls report submitted to the CFO.

7. RECORD RETENTION
Duplicate flags and resolution records must be retained for 7 years.
