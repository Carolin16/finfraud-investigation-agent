ACCOUNTS PAYABLE POLICY — NEW VENDOR ONBOARDING AND HIGH-VALUE INVOICE CONTROLS
Document Reference: AP-POL-004
Issued by: Finance Compliance and Vendor Risk Management
Effective Date: January 2024 | Review Date: January 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy governs the approval of invoices submitted by vendors classified
as new or recently onboarded. New vendors carry elevated fraud risk due to
limited trading history, unverified banking details, and incomplete KYC checks.

2. DEFINITION OF A NEW VENDOR
A vendor is classified as new if:
  — They have been added to the approved vendor list within the last 90 days, or
  — They have fewer than 3 prior approved invoices on record with the firm.

3. HIGH-VALUE THRESHOLD
Any invoice submitted by a new vendor that exceeds $10,000 triggers an
automatic mandatory review. Invoices below $10,000 from new vendors are
still subject to standard three-way match controls.

4. MANDATORY KYC REQUIREMENTS
Before any invoice from a new vendor is approved, the following must be
completed and documented:
  — Company registration verification (Companies House or equivalent)
  — Bank account verification (confirmation that bank details match the
    registered company)
  — Adverse media check (no pending fraud allegations, sanctions, or
    regulatory violations)
  — Procurement sign-off confirming the engagement is legitimate
  — Signed vendor agreement on file

5. APPROVAL AUTHORITY
  — Invoices up to $10,000: AP Manager approval
  — Invoices $10,000 to $50,000: Head of Procurement approval
  — Invoices above $50,000: CFO approval required

6. PAYMENT HOLD PERIOD
All invoices from new vendors with a value above $10,000 are subject to a
minimum 14-day payment hold while KYC and Procurement review are completed.

7. GOODS RECEIPT RECONCILIATION
New vendor invoices must also pass three-way match. Any gap between the
invoice amount and confirmed GR amount above 5% must be escalated.

8. MONITORING PERIOD
New vendors remain under enhanced monitoring for their first 6 months of
trading. Any anomaly during this period triggers an immediate Vendor Risk
review.

9. RECORD RETENTION
All KYC documents, approval records, and vendor correspondence must be
retained for 7 years.
