ACCOUNTS PAYABLE POLICY — INVOICE VARIANCE AND OVERBILLING CONTROLS
Document Reference: AP-POL-001
Issued by: Finance Compliance, Internal Controls Division
Effective Date: January 2024 | Review Date: January 2025

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the controls and escalation procedures for invoices
that exceed contracted purchase order (PO) rates. It applies to all vendor
invoices processed through the AP system.

2. VARIANCE THRESHOLD RULES
Invoices submitted above the contracted PO rate are classified as follows:

  Tier 1 — Minor Variance (up to 5%)
  Invoices may be auto-approved if the deviation does not exceed 5% of the
  contracted PO rate. This accommodates minor rounding, currency fluctuation,
  or nominal freight adjustments. No escalation required.

  Tier 2 — Moderate Variance (5% to 15%)
  Invoices must be reviewed by the AP Manager before payment release.
  The vendor must provide written justification. Payment may be held for up
  to 7 business days pending review.

  Tier 3 — Significant Variance (above 15%)
  Invoices exceeding the contracted PO rate by more than 15% must be escalated
  to the Head of Procurement and CFO sign-off is required before any payment
  is released. The vendor must supply documentary evidence of any rate change,
  including a signed amendment to the original purchase order.

3. THREE-WAY MATCH REQUIREMENT
All invoices must pass a three-way match: invoice amount, PO amount, and
Goods Receipt amount must align within Tier 1 tolerance before payment.
A failed three-way match automatically triggers a payment hold.

4. VENDOR RESPONSIBILITY
Vendors must submit a formal rate amendment request at least 30 days before
submitting invoices at revised rates. Verbal rate change agreements are not
recognised and will result in invoice rejection.

5. ESCALATION PATH
AP Clerk → AP Manager → Head of Procurement → CFO
Each escalation adds up to 5 business days to the payment cycle.

6. RECORD RETENTION
All overbilling flags, vendor communications, and resolution notes must be
retained in the dispute log system for a minimum of 7 years.
